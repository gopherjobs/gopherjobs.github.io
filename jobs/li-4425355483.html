Thermo Fisher Scientific Inc. is the world leader in serving science, with annual revenue of approximately $40 billion. Our Mission is to enable our customers to make the world healthier, cleaner and safer. For more information, please visit www.thermofisher.com.

Thermo Fisher Scientific recently announced that it has entered into an agreement to sell its microbiology business to Astorg, a leading pan-European private equity firm with deep expertise in healthcare, focused on a select set of subsectors, including pharmaceutical services and technology, medtech and related services, life science tools and diagnostics.

This decision follows a thoughtful evaluation as part of Thermo Fisher’s ongoing business strategy to actively manage and strengthen our portfolio.

We expect the transaction will be completed in the second half of 2026, subject to customary closing conditions and applicable regulatory approvals. Until the transaction is complete, Thermo Fisher will continue to operate the business.

If you join the microbiology business prior to the close of the transaction, your employment is expected to transition to Astorg.

How will you make an impact? We are now looking for a Software Engineer to join us.

One of our team members is retiring and thus we are looking for a new person to join us in this role on site in Vantaa.

Joining an international team of professionals, you will contribute to software solutions for microbiology products. Our software is part of regulated in vitro diagnostic laboratory solutions, where quality, reliability, usability, and compliance are essential.

In this role, you will work as part of a software development team building and maintaining applications used in laboratory environments. We mainly develop Windows desktop software today, but we value a broad software engineering mindset and interest in modern software systems, connected solutions, and continuous improvement.

What will you do?

  • Design and develop software solutions: Build and maintain high-quality software for microbiology products.
  • Support modernization and continuous improvement: Help improve existing software, development practices, testability, maintainability, and effective use of modern tools, including AI-based development tooling.
  • Work as part of a cross-functional team: Collaborate with developers, product owners, testers, architects, project managers, quality, regulatory, and other stakeholders to deliver reliable software solutions.
  • Follow quality-driven development practices: Work systematically according to defined development processes, including documentation, traceability, verification, validation, and cybersecurity expectations.

What will you need?

  • Software development experience: Experience designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software applications, preferably on Windows, using technologies such as C#/.NET, Java, or similar.
  • Good engineering fundamentals: Understanding of software design, debugging, source control, automated testing, and collaborative development workflows.
  • Quality-minded way of working: Ability to work systematically, communicate clearly, document your work, and learn domain-specific development practices.
  • Language and education: Proficient communication skills in English. Finnish is considered advantageous. Bachelor’s, Master’s, or equivalent experience in Computer Science, Software Engineering, or a related field.

Seen as a benefit:

  • Experience with clinical microbiology, diagnostics, healthcare software, laboratory workflows, scientific instruments, or IVDR medical device software.
  • Broad software engineering experience from areas such as cloud, web, mobile, or connected software systems.
  • Fluency in, or strong interest in adopting modern AI-based engineering workflows.
  • Experience with cybersecurity, test automation, DevOps, or machine learning solutions.
